Parental Monitoring vs Adolescent Autonomy in Smartphone Use

With the increasing use of smartphones and the internet, parents and guardians are faced with the challenge of protecting their children from the risks associated with digital behaviors.
This has raised the debate about parental control software and the extent to which parents should monitor and limit smartphone use.
Bertrandias et al (2023) continue this by explaining that parents have a responsibility to protect their children from potential harm, and that parental control software can help parents monitor their children's online activity and limit access to inappropriate content. While Bertrandias et al (2023) agree that adolescents have a right to autonomy, Wallace (2021) states that closely watching adolescents can actually intensify their challenging behaviors and that parental supervision, characterized by overly controlling measures, can be harmful.
Overly invasive parental methods can compromise a teen's trust and increase their anxiety, whereas granting them more freedom is linked to reduced depression.

Choice 2: How Excessive Smartphone Use Shapes Adolescents' Relationship

Higher smartphone use can weaken the bond between teenagers and their friends.
This can impact their social growth and their skill in forming strong relationships, which are important during the teenage years (Lim, 2022).
Establishing social relationships during an adolescent's developmental stage helps them learn how to engage in appropriate behaviors in different social environments and establish healthy relationships with different individuals (Lim, 2022).
Huang et al (2022) also add to this by stating adolescents have more opportunities to share information about themselves and spend more time with their friends, which benefits their social relationships.
However, Lim (2022) contrasts this by explaining how excessive smartphone use can lead to difficulties in learning these necessary social skills, hindering the ability to maintain positive relationships with peers.
This can have a detrimental effect on life satisfaction through negative relationships with peers. Therefore, it is important to monitor smartphone use among adolescents and promote healthy social development through positive peer relationships.
