The effects of excessive smartphone use on adolescents' mental health

In modern society, technology is evolving constantly, and digital culture shapes our everyday lives and interactions.
Due to their accessibility, smartphones are now the preferred online device in modern society, making adolescents the most digitally immersed generation yet.

However, despite its benefits, digital connectivity poses challenges. Karsay et al (2019) define excess smartphone use as “overly high cognitive, emotional, and behavioral involvement with the smartphone” (p. 706).
Individuals experience considerable physical, emotional, and social shifts and excessive use of smartphones can amplify these challenges, and as a result, mental health can be significantly impacted.
It’s imperative to mention that adolescence is a vital phase of psychological development, and mental health during this time is crucial (Al-Yateem et al., 2019).

Justifying Topic

Over the past decade, smartphone use has risen rapidly, especially among young adults (Mohd Salleh Sahimi et al., 2022). In New Zealand, smartphones are the preferred device for children at 76%, whereas among teenagers between 15-17, this increases to 93% (Netsafe, 2019). Smartphones have not only revolutionized the lives of adolescents, but also modern society as a whole by providing instant access to information, effortless communication, and in adolescents in particular - a sense of autonomy. However, despite the undeniable benefits, the risks associated with smartphone addiction, particularly its effects on the mental and emotional health of adolescents, are too pressing to overlook. Lacking one can subject youths to bullying and feelings of exclusion. This brings us to the ethical concerns of smartphone use, which include access, privacy, and educational balance.
