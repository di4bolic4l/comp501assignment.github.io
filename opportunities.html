Opportunities

Smartphones, with their ability to connect to the internet, have transformed the way we access information and communicate with others.
This connectivity is vital, enabling global interactions and offering unmatched opportunities for today's communication and learning.


Unlike traditional cell phones, smartphones are also portable but provide access to the internet, enabling users to explore a wide range of apps on a single personal device (Vaterlaus et al., 2021).
Beyond just being a tool for communication and information, this technology acts as a bridge, keeping us connected to the outside world and showing the increasing need to be connected at all times.
Interestingly, while many apps are designed for relaxation and social interactions, it's noteworthy that a significant number of adolescents still value the straightforward connection that even basic phones provide, highlighting the profound importance they place on their social relationships (Vaterlaus et al., 2021; Karsay et al., 2019).
This is further shown in this age group's dominant presence on social media, a space where they find a sense of community, and real-time updates.
Research further emphasises this,suggesting that people with strong online friendships are often drawn to these platforms because they feel a strong connection to the online community (Liubiniene, 2017).
Similarly, Jo & Jang (2022) state that, many adolescents achieve a sense of well-being and address emotional needs, such as their social confidence, by interacting on social media.
